The Inferno

Circle 6

Heresy

Chapter One

The Standoff at the Gates

Before Dante and Virgil can even get to the burning coffins, they are locked out of the City of Dis by fallen angels.

The Visual

Three blood-stained Furies (Megaera, Alecto, and Tisiphone) appear on the flaming high tower, shrieking and tearing at their own chests. They have horned vipers and green hydras for hair.

The Threat

They call for Medusa to come and turn Dante to stone. Virgil literally has to cover Dante’s eyes with his own hands to protect him. This is a massive, high-tension cinematic moment!

Chapter Two

The Heavenly Messenger

Hell’s forces refuse to open the gates, so Heaven intervenes.

The Visual

A terrifying, powerful angel descends from Heaven, walking across the River Styx without his feet ever touching the muck. He scatters the damned souls like frogs, waves a small wand, and blasts the massive gates of Dis open in an explosive shockwave.

Chapter Three

The Sprawling Necropolis

Once inside the walls, it isn't just a few coffins.

The Visual

It is an endless, uneven graveyard. The ground is terrifyingly hot, and the open stone tombs are glowing red like an iron foundry. The flames don't just burn in the coffins; they surround them entirely.

The Audio

The air is filled with the agonizing, overlapping groans and shrieks of the souls trapped inside.

Chapter Four

Farinata the Arrogant

Dante actually speaks to one of the heretics, a political rival named Farinata degli Uberti.

The Visual

Farinata rises from his flaming tomb, visible only from the waist up. His chest and brow are thrust out in sheer defiance, looking around as if he holds all of Hell in absolute contempt. He is so arrogant he doesn't even care that he's burning. It’s a brilliant, imposing character shot.

Chapter Five

The Ultimate Dread

The tragic, theological twist of this circle: the massive, heavy stone lids of these tombs are currently propped open or suspended above the coffins.

The Narrative Punch

Dante is told that after the Final Judgment at the end of time, the heavy stone lids will be slammed shut and sealed forever, plunging the heretics into eternal, burning darkness.
