**Goal:**

Build a self-contained machine learning pipeline to predict Cellular Component (CC) Gene Ontology terms for proteins based strictly on their amino acid sequences. You will use the provided training set of experimentally determined GO terms to train your model. Your ultimate objective is to optimize the maximum information-accretion weighted F1-measure specifically for the CC subontology across no-knowledge, limited-knowledge, and partial-knowledge protein targets. You do not need to consider Molecular Function or Biological Process targets.

This report synthesizes the research and implementation strategies for building a machine learning pipeline to predict Cellular Component (CC) Gene Ontology (GO) terms from protein sequences. The primary objective is to optimize the maximum information-accretion weighted F1-measure (MIAWF1) across varying levels of prior knowledge (no-knowledge, limited-knowledge, and partial-knowledge).

1. Information Accretion (IA) and Data Analysis

The foundation of the evaluation framework rests on Information Accretion (IA) weights. Unlike standard F1-scores, which treat all GO terms as equal, IA-weighted metrics recognize that terms deeper in the Gene Ontology Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) are more specific and provide more information.

The IA for each term is calculated as the negative log-probability of a term occurring given its parents. The root term for Cellular Component (GO:0005575) and its immediate children possess the lowest IA weights. As the model predicts more specific terms, the IA increases, effectively penalizing errors in specific predictions more heavily than errors in generic ones.

A complete mapping of these weights has been established in ia_weights.json to ensure the training objective aligns with biological specificity.

2. Protein Sequence Representation

To transform raw amino acid sequences into a format suitable for deep learning, the pipeline utilizes ESM-2 (Evolutionary Scale Modeling), specifically the esm2_t12_35M_UR50D variant. ESM-2 was selected for its state-of-the-art ability to capture both biophysical properties and evolutionary information directly from sequences without requiring explicit structural data.

The sequence encoding process involves:

  • Mean Pooling: Variable-length protein sequences are passed through the ESM-2 transformer layers, and the last hidden layer is mean-pooled to produce a fixed-size 480-dimensional embedding.
  • Batch Processing: The implementation in sequence_encoder.py supports GPU acceleration and batching to handle large-scale training sets efficiently.

3. Model Architecture: HMM-ResNet

The core of the prediction engine is a Hierarchical Multi-Modal Residual Network (HMM-ResNet). This architecture is specifically designed to handle the complexities of biological ontologies and the varying knowledge levels of target proteins.

Key Architectural Components:

  • Modality Attention Module: This module dynamically manages inputs across different knowledge states. If certain features are missing (common in "no-knowledge" targets), the module uses self-attention to fuse available features and map missing inputs to zero-tensors without crashing the pipeline.
  • Deep Residual Blocks: To prevent vanishing gradients and capture complex non-linear interactions, the model utilizes Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs) with skip connections.
  • Hierarchical Max-Pooling Decoder: This is a critical, parameter-free layer that enforces the True Path Rule of the GO hierarchy. It ensures that if a specific child term is predicted, all its parent terms in the DAG are consistently predicted with at least the same probability.
  • Modality Dropout: During training, this technique simulates missing knowledge states, forcing the model to remain robust when high-level features are unavailable.

4. Evaluation and Knowledge-Level Categorization

The pipeline includes a dedicated MetricsEvaluator class to calculate the maximum information-accretion weighted F1-measure (MIAWF1). This metric is the primary optimization target.

Evaluation Logic:

  • Categorization: Proteins are categorized into three groups based on the sum of IA from existing annotations: no-knowledge, limited-knowledge, and partial-knowledge. This allows for a granular analysis of model performance across different stages of protein characterization.
  • MIAWF1 Calculation: The system computes precision and recall across multiple threshold steps (from 0 to 1). The MIAWF1 score is determined by finding the maximum F1-measure across these thresholds, weighted by the precomputed IA values.
  • Threshold Optimization: The evaluator identifies the optimal probability threshold for term assignment, ensuring the model's predictions are calibrated for maximum biological relevance.
