Limbo

(The Unbaptized & Virtuous Pagans)

Limbo is a place of "sorrow without torment." It’s a green, peaceful meadow with a great citadel, but it is shrouded in an eternal fog of sadness.

The Sin

These souls didn't "sin" in the traditional sense, but they either lived before the time of Christ (like Homer, Socrates, and Aristotle) or were never baptized.

The Punishment

There is no physical pain. Instead, they suffer from spiritual sighing. They live in a state of eternal "longing without hope," knowing that a higher paradise exists but that they are forever barred from it.
