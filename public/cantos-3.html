Circle 3:
Gluttony

Dante finds this circle particularly repulsive—a cold, stinking slush created by "dirty" rain and black snow.

The Sin:

Those who lived for the pleasure of food and drink, ignoring the spiritual for the sake of the belly.

The Punishment:

They lie face down in freezing, putrid muck and filth.

To add to the misery, the three-headed dog Cerberus stands over them, flaying and scratching them with his claws and bark-howling in their ears.

It is a sensory overload of "grossness" to match their overindulgent lives.
