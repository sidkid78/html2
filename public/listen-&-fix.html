The Extortion of the Helpless Consumer

You know the feeling. You’re driving down the highway, and suddenly, your engine starts making a rhythmic clacking noise. Or your refrigerator hums loudly, and the freezer stops freezing.

Instantly, the anxiety hits. It’s not just the stress of a broken machine; it’s the dread of what comes next. It’s the $150 diagnostic fee just to look at it. It’s the fear of a mechanic throwing big words at you—alternator, compressor, torque specs—knowing you have no choice but to hand over your credit card.

For decades, manufacturers and repair shops have held a monopoly on technical knowledge. They hide the manuals. They obscure the symptoms. They weaponize your lack of knowledge against your wallet.

We are ending that today.

The Solution

Shazam

Listen & Fix is an AI-powered diagnostic and repair platform that gives you the knowledge of a master mechanic, right in your pocket.

We’ve built a system that fundamentally changes the power dynamic of ownership. When a machine breaks, you don't call a repairman.

You pull out your phone, point the camera, and let our system listen and look.

Powered by advanced multimodal AI, Listen & Fix doesn't just guess. It acts:

* It Hears and Sees the Invisible: Whether it's a rattling fan belt or a buzzing circuit breaker, the AI analyzes the raw audio and video of your malfunctioning equipment, instantly matching the exact sound and vibration to the root cause.
* It Steals Back the Manuals: While you’re holding your phone, our system goes to work in the background, aggressively hunting down and reading the exact, obscure 500-page manufacturer technical manual for your specific make and model.
* It Guides Your Hands: It doesn't just tell you what's wrong. It generates a custom, step-by-step repair guide tailored strictly to your skill level, the tools you actually own, and your budget.
* It Sources the Parts: Before you even put your phone down, it has already pinged your local AutoZone or Grainger, telling you exactly which $30 part you need to buy to avoid the $800 repair bill.
