Beyond the Guesswork: How Dynamic JIT RAG Eliminates AI Hallucinations in High-Stakes Mechanical Diagnostics

The Problem with Static LLMs in the Workshop

Standard LLMs fail in mechanical diagnostics because they cannot decouple signal from noise. They lack access to granular, machine-specific service bulletins, and they cannot reason across multiple discrete technical steps without losing context. If an operator in a noisy manufacturing plant uploads an audio clip of a failing bearing, a standard LLM will likely offer a generalized, unverified guess.

Instead of relying on pre-trained memory, the engine triggers a Just-In-Time (JIT) RAG pipeline based on the specific equipment metadata detected. The system automatically crawls OEM repositories, cleans the data by stripping out navigational bloat and advertisements, and ingests hyper-relevant technical documentation and service bulletins in real-time.

3. Advanced Vector Embeddings & Multi-Chunk Reasoning

Once the documentation is ingested, our retrieval system utilizes gemini-embedding-2-preview to generate highly optimized, 1536-dimensional vectors. To ensure zero loss of context during cosine similarity searches, we chunk the technical documents at exactly 1,000 characters with a 200-character overlap.

This overlapping multi-chunk reasoning is critical. Mechanical instructions are sequential. By preserving the 200-character context window across chunks, the AI understands that step 4 is dependent on step 3.
