01 // The Reality

The FairGig Manifesto:
The End of the "Pay-to-Play" Era

Every gigging musician knows the feeling.

It’s 2:00 AM. Your ears are ringing, your gear is packed in the van, and you just played your heart out to a packed room. You walk up to the venue owner or promoter to settle up, expecting the payoff for weeks of rehearsal and promotion.

Instead, you get a story.

You get hit with "unforeseen production costs." You get charged a "sound guy fee." You’re handed a sweaty envelope with a fraction of what you were promised, and told to be grateful for the "exposure."

For decades, the live music industry has operated on a rigged system. It thrives on dark alleys, handshake deals, and blind hope. Venues shift all the financial risk onto the artists, relying on the fact that musicians love their craft too much to walk away.

They call it "paying your dues."
We call it theft.

03 // The Simulator

Before You Guess.
Now You Know.

Our core innovation is the Show Economics Simulator. We are replacing shady verbal agreements with cold, hard math. Before you ever load your amp into a venue, FairGig allows you to instantly project exactly what you will make.

  • EMPTY ROOM? Know your exact break-even point.
  • LIKELY CROWD? Lock in your guaranteed split.
  • SOLD OUT? Watch the math prove exactly what you are owed, down to the dollar.

By calculating ticket prices, production fees, and deal structures before the show, we give artists the ultimate leverage: Proof.

You are no longer asking for a fair cut; you are demanding it based on mathematical reality. When the show is over, our automated Settlement System creates a permanent digital receipt. No more disputes. No more missing cash.

04 // The Shift

The Truth Tax:
Forcing the Industry into the Light

We are arming artists with FairGig for free, and it is going to fundamentally shift the power dynamic of local music.

We are creating a world where transparency is the baseline. When a venue or promoter refuses to use FairGig to outline their deals, it sends a massive, glaring red flag to every artist in town.

We call it the "Truth Tax."

Venues have a choice: adopt transparent, fair math, or lose access to the best talent in the city because their rating on our Reputation Layer exposes them as bad actors.
