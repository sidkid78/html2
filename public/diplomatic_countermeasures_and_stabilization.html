Task Overview

Diplomatic Countermeasures & Stabilization

Signal US resolve in Ukraine and Taiwan via multilateral alliances and economic deterrents without requiring a full military pivot from the Middle East.

Theater 01

Middle East Economy of Force

Transition to a localized burden-sharing posture in the Middle East. By leveraging the Abraham Accords framework and empowering regional partners (e.g., Saudi Arabia, UAE, Israel) to take the lead on local maritime security and anti-drone defense networks, the US can free up critical strategic bandwidth and naval assets for the Indo-Pacific and Europe without creating a regional power vacuum.

Theater 02

Ukraine Countermeasures

Multilateral Alliances

Institutionalization of the Ukraine Defense Contact Group (UDCG)

Transition the 'Ramstein format' from an ad-hoc coalition into a formalized, treaty-backed Joint Defense Procurement and Logistics Command. This secures long-term, predictable military aid and signals to Russia that Western support is deeply institutionalized and immune to individual domestic political shifts.

NATO-Ukraine Interoperability and Cyber Shield

Establish multilateral cyber defense and intelligence-sharing hubs in frontline Eastern European states (e.g., Poland, Romania). By integrating Ukrainian officers into joint threat-hunting operations, the US signals deep, structural alliance integration short of formal Article 5 guarantees.

Economic Deterrents

Aggressive Secondary Sanctions on Evasion Networks

Implement targeted secondary sanctions on third-party financial institutions (particularly in the PRC, Central Asia, and the Gulf) that facilitate the transfer of dual-use technologies to Russia's military-industrial complex.

Multilateral Sovereign Asset Confiscation

Coordinate with the G7 and EU to transition from merely utilizing the interest of immobilized Russian sovereign assets to the legal confiscation and transfer of the principal funds into a formalized 'Ukraine Reconstruction and Defense Trust'.

Theater 03

Taiwan Countermeasures

Multilateral Alliances

Expansion of AUKUS Pillar II and Quad Integration

Incorporate regional allies like Japan and South Korea into AUKUS Pillar II (advanced asymmetric capabilities like AI, quantum, and hypersonics). Expand the Quad's Indo-Pacific Maritime Domain Awareness (IPMDA) initiative to implicitly integrate and share early-warning data with Taipei.

G7 Cross-Strait Contingency Planning

Establish a formalized G7 working group dedicated to cross-strait contingencies. This explicitly links European economic security to Indo-Pacific stability, signaling to Beijing a united Western front that transcends regional US bilateral treaties.

Economic Deterrents

Pre-Triggered Multilateral Sanctions Regime ('Economic Article 5')

Codify a pre-negotiated, classified but publicly acknowledged package of severe, automatic capital controls, SWIFT expulsions, and trade embargoes with G7 and EU partners that trigger instantly upon a blockade or kinetic action against Taiwan.

Strategic Tech 'Friend-Shoring' and Supply Chain Weaponization

Leverage CHIPS Act expansions and allied equivalents (e.g., EU Chips Act) to rapidly diversify critical semiconductor and battery supply chains away from mainland China. Simultaneously tighten multilateral export controls on legacy node manufacturing equipment to constrain Chinese market dominance.
