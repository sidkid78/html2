1.The Big Lie of Live Music

The Artist’s Manifesto: Stop Bleeding for the House

Every independent artist knows the feeling. You spent months writing. You rehearsed until your fingers bled. You hauled your gear in the rain, played your heart out, and brought the crowd to life. Then, at 1:00 AM, you walk into the back office to settle up—and the promoter hands you a crumpled twenty-dollar bill.

*"Sound guy was expensive tonight, guys. Door didn't really cover it."*

This is the Big Lie of the live music industry: the illusion that a booked gig is a fair gig. For decades, a parasitic system has thrived by exploiting your passion. Venues and shady promoters use your art as a shield, offloading 100% of the financial risk onto your shoulders while taking the lion's share of the reward. They gamble with your money, and when they lose, you pay the bill.

You aren't operating a business. You are being used as unpaid labor to keep their lights on.

2.The Weapon of Financial Truth

You cannot fight a shadow. As long as venue costs are hidden, verbal agreements are vague, and "production fees" remain a mystery, you will always be the victim of the payout.

To take back your power, you don't need to become an accountant. You need a weapon. That weapon is Financial Truth.

When a fan buys a ticket, that money doesn't belong to the venue—it belongs to the show. But before a single dollar reaches your pocket, it is scavenged. Ticketing fees. House bills. Mysterious "marketing" costs. If you cannot see exactly where every penny is going, you are already being robbed.

By demanding absolute, mathematical transparency, you force the industry into the light. You stop hoping for a fair payout and start demanding it.

3.The Line in the Sand

You should never load an amp until you know your Break-Even point. This isn't just a number; it is your line in the sand. It is the exact number of tickets required to cover the house costs.

If a venue hides their costs, inflates their room fees, or refuses to give you the math upfront, they are waving a massive red flag. They aren't looking for a partner; they are looking for a sponsor.

We built FairGig so you never have to guess again. We simulate the economics of the room before you ever sign the contract. We show you the floor, the realistic crowd, and the sold-out ceiling. If your known draw can't cover the venue's bloated overhead, you don't beg for the gig. You walk away. You take your fans to a venue that respects your craft.

4.The Truth Tax

For too long, bad venues operated in the dark. No longer.

By uniting artists through verified, data-backed reputations, we are enforcing a Truth Tax on the live music industry. Venues must now choose: operate with total transparency and fairness, or lose access to quality artists entirely.

If a venue has a history of changing the math after the doors close, the FairGig network will flag them. We are replacing the old boys club of "who you know" with a cartel of empowered artists who protect their own.

5.Protecting the Sanctuary

Music is not just a hustle. As veteran musicians like Cliff Franco learned after 15+ years of fighting it out in bands like The Pursuit and The Struggs, music is a personal sanctuary. It is a sacred space that deserves to be protected from the grinding, predatory nature of the industry.

But you cannot protect your sanctuary with blind hope. You protect it with cold, hard data.

When you master the math of the room, the anxiety disappears. You stop wondering if you're being scammed. You stop worrying about the settlement at the end of the night. You take command, demand what you are worth, and return your focus to the only thing that ever mattered: the music.

Real Gigs. Real Pay.
Never play in the dark again.
