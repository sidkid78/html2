INITIATING FRAMEWORK

Protein Function Prediction: Architectural and Evaluation Framework

Executive Summary

This briefing document synthesizes technical specifications, research findings, and strategic planning for a machine learning pipeline designed to predict Gene Ontology (GO) terms. The primary objective is to maximize predictive accuracy across three subontologies—Molecular Function (MF), Biological Process (BP), and Cellular Component (CC)—using an Information-Accretion (IA) weighted framework.

Fundamental Shifts Required

  • Metric Realignment: Shifting from unweighted F1-scores to Information-Accretion (IA) weighted Fmax. This prioritizes the prediction of rare, granular, and biologically informative terms over generic, high-level classifications.
  • Representation Depth: Leveraging Protein Language Models (PLMs) like ProtT5-XL and ESM-2 to extract dense, context-aware embeddings that capture evolutionary and structural features directly from amino acid sequences.
  • Hierarchical Integrity: Enforcing the True Path Rule (where child term probability cannot exceed parent term probability) through custom loss functions and hierarchical decoders.

The recommended strategy for the CAFA 6 competition involves a unified pipeline using an ESM-2 (650M) backbone, specialized subontology heads (HMM-ResNet and PLM-GCN hybrids), and a knowledge-tiered evaluation framework to address "dark matter" proteins (those with no prior annotations).

END SUMMARY
PHASE I: METRIC CALIBRATION

I. Information-Accretion (IA) Weighting Framework

Standard classification metrics are identified as biologically flawed because they treat all GO terms as equal. The IA framework addresses this by weighting terms based on their specificity within the Gene Ontology Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG).

1. Calculation Methodology

The informativeness of a term v is quantified by the information gain relative to its parents pa(v):

Formula: ia(v) = -log_2(P(v | pa(v)))
  • Root Terms: Assigned a weight of 0.0 (e.g., GO:0003674).
  • Granular Terms: Assigned higher weights due to lower frequency and higher specificity (e.g., GO:0008289 has a weight of 5.87).

2. Evidence Code Integration

To maintain data integrity, the pipeline applies multipliers to the IA weights based on the quality of evidence:

  • Experimental Evidence: Multiplier of 1.0.
  • Electronic Annotation (IEA): Scaled down (multiplier < 1.0).
  • Conflict Resolution: If multiple evidence codes exist for a single association, the maximum weight is utilized to prioritize the most informative known annotation.

3. Weighted Metrics

Performance is measured via IA-weighted Precision and Recall:

  • Weighted Precision (Pr_{IA}): Sum of IA weights of correctly predicted terms divided by the sum of weights of all predicted terms.
  • Weighted Recall (Rc_{IA}): Sum of IA weights of correctly predicted terms divided by the sum of weights of all true terms.
  • Fmax: The maximum weighted F1-measure across all possible decision thresholds.

2. Preprocessing and Extraction Workflow

  • Sanitization: Non-standard amino acids (U, Z, B, O) are mapped to 'X'. Sequences shorter than 30 residues are filtered out.
  • Handling Length Limits: Standard PLMs have a limit of 1024 residues. For longer multi-domain proteins, a sliding window strategy (e.g., 1024 window, 512 stride) is employed, with resulting embeddings averaged.
  • Pooling Strategy: Global Average Pooling (Mean Pooling) across the residue dimension of the last hidden layer is the most effective method for capturing global functional context.
END PHASE II
PHASE III: STRUCTURAL TOPOLOGY

III. Architectural Design for Multi-Label Prediction

The core predictive engine is a hybrid network designed to handle varied input types and hierarchical constraints.

1. Hierarchical Multi-Modal Residual Network (HMM-ResNet)

This architecture is specifically designed for cases where input completeness varies:

  • Modality Attention Module: Processes available data (sequence, secondary structure, or PPI node embeddings). Missing modalities are replaced with zero-tensors or [MASK] tokens.
  • Deep Residual Blocks: 3 to 4 blocks (Linear -> LayerNorm -> GELU -> Dropout) with skip connections prevent vanishing gradients.

2. Specialized Subontology Heads

  • Molecular Function (MF): Utilizes a PLM-GCN Hybrid. If 3D structural data (AlphaFold/PDB) is available, a Graph Convolutional Network processes structural contact maps to refine residue embeddings.
  • Biological Process (BP): Employs a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) with BatchNorm1d and InformationAccretionLoss to scale penalties for misclassifying rare terms.
  • Cellular Component (CC): Uses the Hierarchical Max-Pooling Decoder, a parameter-free layer that resolves parent probability as the maximum of its own probability and that of its children.

3. Advanced Loss Functions

  • Addressing Imbalance: Asymmetric Loss (ASL) or Focal Loss is used to down-weight the influence of easy negative examples in sparse multi-label datasets.
  • Hierarchical Regularization: A custom loss penalty is applied if a child term is assigned a higher probability than its parent, enforcing the biological logic of the GO hierarchy.
END PHASE III
PHASE IV: STRATEGIC DEPLOYMENT

IV. Evaluation and Target Stratification

To ensure robustness, targets are stratified based on their experimental annotation status using a temporal split (T_0 for training, T_1 for testing).

Category
Definition
Evaluation Goal
No-Knowledge (NK)

Limited-Knowledge (LK)
Zero annotations in the target ontology at T_0, but annotations exist in others.
Test ability to leverage cross-ontology correlations.
Partial-Knowledge (PK)
Existing annotations in the target ontology at T_0 that accumulate more at T_1.
Test ability to refine or extend specific sub-term profiles.

V. Strategic Unified Pipeline for CAFA 6

The synthesized research recommends a consolidated strategy to optimize compute and performance:

  • Unified Shared Backbone: Standardize on ESM-2 650M as the global encoder to eliminate redundant feature extraction and manage VRAM constraints (16–24GB limits).
  • Shared Master Configuration: Centralize IA weights and GO DAG relationships into a single master file (ia_weights_master.json) for all subontology heads.
  • Global Hierarchical Enforcement: Port the Hierarchical Max-Pooling Decoder to all heads (MF, BP, CC) to guarantee that inference results never violate the True Path Rule.
  • Resilience via Modality Dropout: Apply Modality Dropout during training to ensure the model remains functional even when 3D structural data or domain features are missing.
