A Historical Juxtaposition

The Lewis Huggins Leopards

A Monument to a Quiet Hero

In the mid-20th century, the world was held hostage by a microscopic terror. Polio was paralyzing a generation of children, filling hospital wards with iron lungs and suffocating communities in fear. Behind the scenes of this global nightmare, a small team of scientists at the University of Pittsburgh worked relentlessly to engineer a miracle.

Dr. L. James Lewis was one of those quiet heroes.

Working directly alongside Dr. Jonas Salk, Dr. Lewis spent grueling, endless hours in sterile laboratories typing virus strains and conducting the critical testing that would eventually prove the Salk Polio Vaccine was safe. He helped give the world its future back.

But outside the sterile, high-stakes environment of the laboratory, Dr. Lewis was a man captivated by the wild, vibrant beauty of the world he was working so hard to save. "The Lewis Huggins Leopards" is the breathtaking physical manifestation of that passion.

Chapter 01

The Artifact

Acquired during Dr. Lewis’s travels to South Africa—a journey echoing the global triumph of the very vaccine he helped create—this piece is a masterwork of scale and grandeur. At its center is a striking, large-scale lithographic reproduction of William Huggins’ 1845 masterpiece, Two Leopards.

Huggins, known as the "Landseer of Liverpool," captured the untamed majesty of these predators with an obsessive attention to detail that mirrors the precision of Dr. Lewis’s own scientific mind.

Chapter 02

The Presentation

But it is the presentation that elevates this piece into the realm of the extraordinary.

The artwork is encased in a massive, custom-built 55x50-inch frame utilizing the rare and painstaking technique of Verre Églomisé. Master artisans applied delicate geometric gold leaf entirely in reverse to the back of the glass. The result is a luminous, glowing border that traps the light, making the massive frame as much of an artistic triumph as the leopards it surrounds.

Chapter 03

**The Legacy**

To the casual observer, this is a spectacular, high-end vintage statement piece. But to the collector of history, it is something entirely different.

This piece hung in the home of a man who changed the course of human medicine. It is a striking juxtaposition: the ferocious, untamed leopards encased in delicate gold, collected by a scientist who conquered a deadly virus.

Chapter 04

Custodianship and Valuation

An artifact of this magnitude—combining world-class Verre Églomisé craftsmanship with ironclad provenance to the Salk vaccine team—is rarely offered to the public. Valued between $1,800 and over $3,200, its true worth transcends the materials. It requires a specific caliber of collector.

Because of the delicate, reverse-applied gold leaf, it cannot endure harsh chemicals or careless handling; it must be preserved with the same gentle precision Dr. Lewis applied to his life's work.

To acquire the Lewis Huggins Leopards is not merely to buy a piece of art. It is to become the custodian of a legacy, preserving a beautiful fragment of the life of a man who helped save the world.
