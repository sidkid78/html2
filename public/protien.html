Protein Sequence Preprocessing

and Feature Extraction Layer

1. Scope

This specification details the design and implementation of the sequence preprocessing and feature extraction layer optimized for predicting Gene Ontology Molecular Function (MF) terms.

2. Selected Embedding Strategy

Following a comparative analysis of One-hot+CNN, ESM-2, and ProtT5, the ProtT5-XL (encoder-only) architecture is selected as the primary feature extractor for maximum predictive accuracy in multi-label classification. ESM-2 (t33_650M_UR50D) is designated as the fallback for environments with constrained computational resources.

3. Sequence Preprocessing Workflow

3.1 Data Sanitization

  • Canonicalization: Map non-standard amino acids (U, Z, B, O) to 'X'.
  • Filtering: Remove sequences shorter than 30 residues to avoid noise from fragments.
  • Format: Input strings in FASTA or direct sequence arrays.

3.2 Tokenization

  • Use model-specific tokenizers (T5Tokenizer for ProtT5; EsmTokenizer for ESM-2).
  • Add specific tokens: <cls> or <s> for start-of-sequence and </s> or <eos> for end-of-sequence as required by the model.
  • Spaces: For ProtT5, add a space between each amino acid residue (e.g., "M E T...").

3.3 Batching and Padding

  • Dynamic Padding: Pad sequences within a batch to the length of the longest sequence in that batch using zero-padding.
  • Max Length Handling: Hard limit at 1024 residues for standard inference. For sequences >1024, apply a sliding window (1024 window, 512 stride) and average the resulting embeddings.

4.2 Layer Selection & Pooling

  • Extraction Layer: Extract representations from the last hidden layer.
  • Pooling Strategy: Global Average Pooling (Mean Pooling) across the residue dimension (ignoring pad/special tokens).
  • Resulting Vector: A single fixed-size dense vector representing the global functional context of the protein.

5. Implementation Requirements

5.1 Software Dependencies

  • python >= 3.8
  • pytorch >= 1.12
  • transformers >= 4.20
  • fair-esm (for ESM-2 support)

5.2 Hardware Targets

  • Inference (ProtT5-XL): NVIDIA A100 (40GB/80GB) or RTX 3090/4090 (24GB) using half() precision.
  • Inference (ESM-2 650M): NVIDIA RTX 3060 (12GB) or higher.
  • Latency Target: < 50ms per protein (batched) on modern GPUs.

6. Efficiency vs. Performance

  • ProtT5-XL: ~0.80 Fmax (MF); Highest memory requirement.
  • ESM-2 (650M): ~0.76 Fmax (MF); High efficiency, compatible with standard workstations.
  • CNN (One-hot): ~0.50 Fmax (MF); Baseline performance, only suitable for ultra-low latency requirements.
